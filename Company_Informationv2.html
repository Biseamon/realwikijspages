Blueberry

Blueberry is a UK based software development company. There are three companies in our group:

  • Blueberry Consultants - http://bbconsult.co.uk
  • Blueberry Systems - http://blueberrysystems.co.uk/
  • Blueberry Software – http://bbsoftware.co.uk

Blueberry Consultants and Blueberry Systems both undertake contract development for many UK companies, departments connected to Local and Central Government as well as many entrepreneurs. Blueberry has been in business since 1998 completing over 200 projects.

Blueberry Software develops and sells the FlashBack range of screen recorder products. We have been doing this for over 10 years.

Blueberry is unusual in a number of ways: 

  • Everyone here is technical – the CEO, Martin Green, was a developer from the age of 14, and he remains hands-on with technology. The managers are all ex-developers.
  • We have a wide team of people – Over 30 in the UK and over 75 are spread across the world with the majority located in Russia, Belarus and Kazakhstan but we also have individuals based in South America.
  • Everyone works directly for the UK company – we do not have any representative bodies or agencies outside the UK.  Instead, developers work from home or sometimes a small local office like the one we have in Rostov.
  • We are very flexible about working patterns, and we always pay on time.
  • We have a simple company structure – all the companies are 100% owned by the CEO – there are no other shareholders to demand we do things differently. This means that if we need to do something differently, or there is an urgent problem, it can be solved very rapidly.

Blueberry's mission is simple: we exist to develop good software, and (ideally) to improve the world by doing so. Although most of our business is custom software, we are also developing a few new products which we hope will take us closer to this goal.

Blueberry is financially stable – we have good customers, and reserves. 

Birmingham Office (HQ) 

The Birmingham office for Blueberry (Blueberry Consultants) is located within a former Victorian Industrial Factory on the Grand Union Canal. The name 'The Bond' originates from a time when the facility was used as a 'Bonded' warehouse in which dutiable (no duty was paid) goods were stored, manipulated or underwent a manufacturing process. In its hey-dey, tea was the main goods that passed through this facility.

HQ Faxing

We have two ways of sending faxes. You can use the dusty old Brother fax machine at the back of the office, or you can use Switchvox to send faxes for you.

Using Switchvox

Open the devices panel in Windows Control Panel and click "Add a printer".
 


 Click "The printer that I wanted isn't listed".
 


Enter the details as shown.
 


Enter your extension number and your extension password.
 

Select the "Generic" manufacturer and the "MS Publisher Color Printer" printer.
 



That's it; you can now print to the device "faxprinter" on your computer, and it'll send it to your Switchvox Fax.Drafts folder. Click here to access it.

HQ Printing

Adding the Brother Printer

  1. Download the appropriate driver from below:

    Windows 7 32-bit - Download
    Windows 7 64-bit - Download
    Windows 8.1 32-bit - Download
    Windows 8.1 64-bit - DownloadFor any other operating system, please visit the Brother download page hereRun the installer.
  2. Run the Installer
  3. On the Connection Type screen, select Wired Network Connection.
  4. On the next screen ('Select Connection'), select Brother Peer-to-Peer Network Printer.Note - if a warning popup appears, simply click No to dismiss it.
  5. On the Network Search Type screen, select Specify your machine be address (Advanced Users) and enter 10.16.1.96 as the IP Address.
  6. When all drivers have been installed, open the Control Panel and go to View Devices and Printers.
  7. In the Printer section, if the Brother print has not appeared, right click and select Add a Printer.
  8. Click Add a network, wireless or Bluetooth printer.
  9. Select Brother HL-4150CDN series.
  10. Print a test page to make sure that everything is working correctly.

Using the Canon A3 Laser Printer

To print on the Canon Laser Printer, there are 4 ways of doing this.

Method 1: You need to log on to the print server and then print your file from there. You can do this by:

RDP to 'print-server' using the credentials found in BitWarden (under 'HQ Print Server')

Copy your file to the print server

Open your file and select Print

Select Canon Pro 9500 as the printer

Method 2:

Open the Network folder from file explorer

Find PRINT-SERVER

Enter PRINT-SERVER

Click Canon Printer

Method 2B:

If Method 2 fails, try right clicking the Canon Printer and choosing connect.

The printer should now be installed and selectable from Any Print Dialog Window.

Method 3:

If the PRINT-SERVER cannot be found, this may be a network issue that should be raised with sysadmins.

If they cannot offer any immediate solution or are unavailable, you can use the newly installed monitor, keyboard and mouse on the PRINT-SERVER computer, located to the right of the Canon 9500 Printer.

You can take your file on a USB drive, open it inside the PRINT-SERVER PC and print directly from there, but normally you should be able to print directly from any machine.

Method 4:

If it is not a network issue and the sysadmins are unavailable, and the PRINT-SERVER PC is broken (indicated by right side fan not spinning when attempting to switch back on) and it is very very urgent, then you can connect your laptop directly to the printer by removing the USB cable located in the back of the PRINT-SERVER PC and installing the printer directly to your machine.

HQ Scanning

There are two scanners at HQ, a flatbed Canon and a sheet-fed scanner.

Support Process

 


Stonehouse Fire Procedures

In the event of a fire, raise the alarm using the nearest call point (one at the top and bottom of the staircase)

On hearing the alarm:

Fire warden will lead the fire evacuation and call 999

Leave the building calmly and promptly and gather at the assembly point in the car park. DO NOT re enter the building until advised its safe to do so.

The escape route is down the stairs and out the main door. 

Fire warden will conduct a roll call and sweep of the building and close all doors on the way out

Please ensure all Visitors are signed in using the visitor book downstairs and in the event of the alarm sounding, the meeting head is responsible for taking their visitors to the meeting point.

Note:

Please do not attempt to fight a fire unless you feel it is safe to do so (i.e. clear means of escape etc.)

Please do not pick up personal belongings. Exit the building as promptly as possible.

Every Friday we will test the alarm, we let you know in advance of doing this. 

There will be regular fire drills conducted, please follow the instructions above and treat it as a real scenario.

Any queries, please contact either Lynn Roome or Harry Holdstock
