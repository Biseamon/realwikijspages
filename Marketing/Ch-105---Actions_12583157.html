Blueberry : Ch. 10.5 - Actions

Created by Stelios Dritsas on Jul 04, 2016

After setting your enrolment properties, you can now create ‘actions’ and ‘timings’ for your workflow to execute. To do this, select the ‘Define this action’ text located underneath the enrolment options (shown below).

Then from the newly presented drop-down menu called ‘select an option’, choose and configure your workflow actions whilst also remembering to save! From here the process now involves you implementing yout prepared workflow actions and timings, but for reference, please see the list below for the most popular choices.

  • Delay: Set a delay between workflow actions

  • If/then branch: Choose a yes/no contact property that splits user, but remember to complete both sides of the branching!

  • Send an email: Send an automated email to the contact going through a workflow

After you have set an action, you can then generate a proceeding one by using the text for ‘Add action or delay’ (as shown above), then repeat as necessary. You can then end the workflow by leading an action up to the ‘Workflow end’ text (below).

Once all actions have been completed, test the workflow thoroughly (by enrolling yourself and completing each stage) using the ‘Test’ link located towards the upper right-hand corner.

However, just before you proceed to activate your workflow, access the workflows ‘Settings’ from the last option of the left-hand sidebar.

Most of the options here are self-explanatory, but it’s worth taking a closer look at the options for Suppressions & Priorities. Again, you shouldn’t have any need to change these settings, but depending on what type of workflow you have created, edit as necessary.

Now you can switch on your workflow by using activation slider (below), which will publish just after before completing the pop-up for last minute checks.
