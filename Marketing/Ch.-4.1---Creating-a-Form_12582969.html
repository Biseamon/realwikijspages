Blueberry : Ch. 4.1 - Creating a Form

Created by Stelios Dritsas, last modified on Jun 30, 2016

To create a Form, navigate to the dashboard and click Contacts > Forms. Please note, you can also read HS’s own guidance on this subject here.

To create a new Form, click the blue button located towards the top-right hand side of the page called ‘Create new form’.

A ‘Create new form’ pop-up will now appear asking you to name your form (for internal use), but be as descriptive as possible, and when finished press ‘Create form’. You’ll now be redirected to the ‘Details’ tab of the main form creator. From here, enter actionable text for your ‘Submit Button Text’ (underneath Form Name), then select the ‘Fields’ tab.

Be aware, you can check how your form looks at any time by pressing the ‘Preview’ button located towards top-middle of the page (below).

From the ‘Fields’ tab, select the fields you need either by using the search-bar, or by expanding the category’s on the far-left by pressing the arrow button, then dragging and dropping the relevant fields into the ‘Default questions’ section (below). You can also drag a ‘Add separator’ across to give each field a custom header.

To edit the fields in your ‘Default questions’ section (below), click the ‘Edit field options’ (pencil icon) button towards the right-hand side of your questions, and a pop-up named ‘Editing Field for Property’ will open.

From this pop-up, you will have options for the following:

      1. Field Configuration

    1. Make field required: The form cannot be submitted without the user having completed this field.

    2. Make field hidden: Hides the field from the user, for internal category setting usage. For example, you could set a hidden ‘persona’ field to support categorisation later on.

    3. Label: The label used for the form field.

    4. Placeholder: Text that appears in the field before anything is typed.

    5. Help Text: Text presented below the field to help the user.

    6. Default Value: Enters a default value into the value, that unless changed by the user will be submitted with the form.

When finished press ‘Done Editing’.

You can also tag your fields as ‘Smart’ by pressing the star icon (below) to the far-right of your fields.

Selecting this option will hide this field if the user has previously answered this question or if the property already has a value on their contact record. The email field will always remain visible on the form, but will be pre-populated with the data from previous submissions.

Next, select the ‘Options’ tab, and complete the following:

  1. Pre-populate fields with known values: Completes the field if the user has known values on their contact record. It’s better practice to use the Smart Fields feature for this.
  2. Disable cookie tracking: When disabled, every submission from the same browser creates a new contact. Be aware, if selected, you cannot use the Smart Fields feature, and page information such as page view history and source data will not be available.
  3. Display Captcha on this form? Display a Captcha on this form to prevent automated form submissions.

       4. Notified when submitted: Entered email addresses will be sent a notification when the form is completed.

 Complete these steps until you have a form you are happy with, then when ready press the blue ‘Save Form’ button towards the top-right of the page.
