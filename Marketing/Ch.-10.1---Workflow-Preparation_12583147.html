Blueberry : Ch. 10.1 - Workflow Preparation

Created by Stelios Dritsas, last modified by Unknown User (mct) on Jul 07, 2016

HS describes Workflows as the following: “A workflow is a series of automated actions that you can trigger to occur based on a person’s behaviours or contact information. Workflows go way beyond simple marketing automation, though. With workflows, you can send emails, update contact information, add or remove contacts from lists, and trigger email notifications.”

Therefore to create a Workflow, it’s best to prepare the following elements in advance:

  1. Have a clear objective(s) for your workflow.

  2. Identify the HS segmentation properties or the list membership to enrol your audience.

  3. Identify the HS segmentation properties that will track the completion of your main workflow objective(s) – these may differ from your enrolling requirements.

  4. Draw a flow-chart with a pencil and paper, or try www.lucidchart.com.

  5. Create your workflow content (usually emails). For instructions, please refer to the chapter 7.


As an example, here’s how we could employ the five actions above:

  • We have the objective of getting new CMM contacts to configure an AWS server

  • We identify that our audience is segmented by the ‘Free Trial Contact List’

  • We also identify that we can track the main workflow objective through the HS contact property ‘Configured Server’

  • For clarity, we then create a flow-chart with the following actions and timings (below)

    1. Detail the list or segmentation properties of our audience

    2. Send our segmented CMM audience the ‘Configure Server’ email

    3. Add a 3 day delay

    4. We then use the ‘Configured Server’ contact property to track if our users actually configured a server

    5. If Yes - we send them a new ‘Configure Servers to be On-Demand’ email

    6. If No - we resend then another ‘Configure Server’ email

  • We would then proceed to create this new Workflow within HS

 

However, when creating a new workflow for real; the area that will prove most difficult to users with a low-level of HS knowledge, is making sure to always use the correct HS contact properties. To which there is no shortcut other than familiarising yourself with them.

The best way to achieve this, is to create a test-list for your workflow within the Contacts section, and experiment with contact properties until you’re 100% sure that you’re pulling through the correct user information. For instructions on how to create a list, please see chapter 11.2.
