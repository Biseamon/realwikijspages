What benefits does it provide?

The biggest benefit of Wistia right now for Blueberry is that it gives us stats, integrates nicely with the website (we have the ability to change the colour of the play bar etc. to make it fit in with the brand) and it tells us how much of a video is being watched by people.

Where the enhanced video stats are particularly useful is to see which videos are being watched, and how long people are watching for - i.e  only watching for 20 seconds and then clicking off. We'll know that people aren't engaged with the content

Wistia basically sets you apart from YouTube and Vimeo as being more of a business tool as opposed to a general video hosting site. We could effectively put all of our video content on there.


What analytics does it provide?

  1. You can see if a HS contact has viewed a video from their timeline

  2. You can segment HS contacts based upon what videos they have watched

  3. You can see which videos help drive conversions

  4. Provides heat mapping - how much and what parts of a video are being watched

  5. See where users click on the video (annotations and CTA’s)

  6. You can include videos within our emails campaigns

  7. You can use smart-lists to identify and segment users into a workflow, for those who have watched a video 100% etc or by many times they’ve viewed it

  8. You can run reports to see how HubSpot videos are performing, and how they affect conversion rates


SEO

Video SEO is when videos show up in Google in the form of rich snippets results, where they would display a video thumbnail, making them better at generating traffic. Also, pages that host a video are more likely to get found in search engines because google ranks them higher.

However, these days video content is less frequently shown in search, and only the most credible websites or Youtube seem to get ranked. All of which can change at Google’s discretion.

The way you rank a video with SEO, is by using a sitemap and the correct keyword metadata for your video with descriptions and transcript information, all of which you can do with a Wistia account.

Assessment

Wistia seems like a nice and useful addition to HS, but it's not something DD are pushing as crucial for CMM (at least), and it would cost us $3,600 per annum - for all domains.

Having the analytics described would be very productive for videos that encourage a signup, but at present these videos are in limited use, and the majority of videos are post-sale and support based.

If implemented now, Wistia could tell us how many sign-ups watched a video, and for how long before registering. We could then create a conversion rate for those that have watched a video, against those who didn’t, and base further video development around this.

However without any video metrics, it’s impossible to tell whether any of our existing videos make a difference to conversions. It may be useful to implement a youtube player as a pilot test (as Dave has suggested), and see how many sign-ups complete the goal of watching the video.

Or perhaps a faster option is implement Wistia for a limted time (maybe 2 -3 months), once the rate of sign-ups has increased (CMM, maybe not relevant to FB), and we can make more of an informed decision towards it’s permanent usage.

In CMM’s case, I would see further video content focusing on our target customer personas, and higher funnel content like webinars. Whereas for FB and consult, videos might be more generalised.

However, if we did implement Wistia on a permanent basis,  there’d have to be a greater focus on video development for a larger ROI, which would pull attention away from other areas such as content which forms our marketing backbone.


Conclusion

In conclusion, video content does not seem to be as prominent as it once was in google search, but more than likely will still help web pages rank higher. 

Additionally,because we don't have to many promotional videos at the moment (to my knowledge), Wistia is probably not worth the cost at present, but is perhaps something we should revisit after our text-based content marketing has truly taken effect. To where we can create videos targeted more towards our marketing personas, and integrate them into higher funneled-content workflows.
