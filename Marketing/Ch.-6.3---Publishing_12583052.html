Blueberry : Ch. 6.3 - Publishing

Created by Stelios Dritsas, last modified on Jul 01, 2016

The publishing page is useful because it shows how effective previous social updates have been, and allows users to craft better messages as a result.

The main view of the page will show how many clicks and interactions each past update has had. More specifically, clicks indicate (ideally) how many people have clicked the link or image within the message, whilst interactions will include Twitter responses such as retweets, likes, and replies.

The filters for this publishing section are very straight forward.

The ‘Published’ and ‘Scheduled’ tabs will simply show what they describe, whilst the ‘Unsuccessful’ tab will display those updates that failed to auto-publish, with an explanation as to why. In more cases this is very rare, but if this happens, simply reschedule the failed message for another time.

In addition, the ‘Filter Channels’ tab lets you segment social updates by different accounts like Facebook and LinkedIn, the ‘All campaigns’ tab segments by HS campaign, and the ‘All time’ tab is by time period.
