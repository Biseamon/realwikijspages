Blueberry : Ch. 5.4 - Entering Blog Text

Created by Stelios Dritsas, last modified by Unknown User (mct) on Jul 07, 2016

Enter the main text for your blog post under the ‘Edit’ subsection.

For instructions on how to enter your text, please refer to chapter 1 - Content Editor.

To start inserting your main body text, simply click on the slightly greyed out sections with the circular pencil icons, called ‘Title’ and ‘Post Body’ (shown below).

For the ‘Title’ section, simply click on the box shown below.

For the ‘Post Body’ section, simply click on the box shown below.

Then whilst entering your ‘Post Body’ text, complete these additional actions to optimize the effectiveness of your blog post:

  1. Spacing: Delete any extra spaces created between paragraphs.

  2. Insert a Read More Separator: The read more separator tells HubSpot where to stop the preview text within the main blogging webpage (shown below). 


    To do this, place the text cursor after an opening paragraph (usually the second), then press the ‘insert read more separator’ within the text toolbar (below).



  3. Linking: Every link you include should open within a new window. 

    To do this, highlight you chosen text and click the ‘insert/edit link’ button within the text toolbar, add your URL and then tick the ‘open in a new window’ box shown within the pop-up.

  4. Images: Each blog post should contain at least one image, which needs to be positioned towards the beginning of the article, before the ‘read more separator’. 

    Hopefully, you’ve already uploaded an image in advance for you blog post, but just in case you haven’t, please refer back to the ‘Uploading an Image’ section within chapter 2. 

    To insert your image, place the text cursor in the desired image location and click the ‘insert/edit image’ button within the text toolbar to open a pop-up. Next, navigate through the ‘Select an image’ pop-up to find your specific image, then insert it using the blue ‘use image’ button towards the bottom left. 

    Once inserted, right align the image, and then (depending on its size) change its size to suit the article by double clicking the image and selecting the ‘Edit Image…’ button. Then within the immediate pop-up, reduce the dimensions until the desired fit is achieved. Alternatively, select one of the quick-size options available when double clicking the image.

  5. Call to Actions (CTA’s): A single CTA should be used to finish every blog post, to redirect the audience to a relevant CMM page. 

    Hopefully, you’ve already uploaded a CTA in advance for you blog post, but just in case you haven’t, please refer back to chapter 3. 

    To insert a CTA into your blog post, place the text cursor next to the desired CTA location and click the ‘Insert Call-to-Action’ button (star shaped icon) within the text toolbar. From this location, select the specific CTA needed, and insert it by selecting the blue ‘Insert CTA’ button towards the bottom left.
