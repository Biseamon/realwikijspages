Blueberry : Ch. 1.1 - The Text Editor Toolbar

Created by Unknown User (mct), last modified on Jul 07, 2016

To edit the content of your page hover over a module until it turns light grey and click the pencil icon in the centre of the box. When the module is open you can now enter or edit your text using the ‘Text Editor Toolbar’ (shown below).

Icons from left to right:

  1. Undo: Undo your last action.
  2. Redo: Repeat your past action.
  3. Source code: View HTML source code.
  4. Spellcheck: Spellcheck highlighted text.
  5. Bold: Bold highlighted text.
  6. Italic: Change highlighted text into italics.
  7. Text colour: Change colour of highlighted text.
  8. Background colour: Change background colour of highlighted text.
  9. Align left: Align text left.
  10. Align centre: Align text centre.
  11. Alight right: Align text right.
  12. Bullet list: Insert bullet points.
  13. Numbered list: Insert numbered points.
  14. Decrease indent: Decrease text indent.
  15. Increase indent: increase text indent.

  16. Insert/edit link: Press this button to insert a hyperlink via highlighted text:
    1. To direct your audience to an external webpage, paste your URL into the ‘Link URL’ box.
    2. For internal linking, press the drop menu for ‘Your pages’, then type in the name of your page and select.
    3. Select either of the two checkboxes underneath the ‘Link URL’ box as needed, then press 'Add link'.

  17. Insert/edit image: Press this button to insert a prepared image:
    1. To create an image please refer to chapter 2.
    2. To insert an image, place your text cursor in the desired module location and press the ‘Insert/edit image’ button to open a pop-up
    3. Next, scroll through or use the search bar within the ‘Select an image’ pop-up to find your specific image and select it.
    4. Insert it using the blue ‘use image’ button towards the bottom-left.
    5. To upload an image via this pop-up, click the ‘Upload a file…’ button, then complete the above steps from C to D.

  18. Insert/edit video: Press this button to insert a video:
    1. To insert a video, place your text cursor in the desired module location and press the ‘Insert/edit video’ button to open a pop-up.
    2. Next, under the ‘General’ tab paste in your video’s URL and choose your video's dimensions.
    3. Or, paste in an embedded code under the ‘Embedded’ tab.
    4. To finish, click ‘Okay’.

  19. Insert Document Link: Press this button to insert a document link:
    1. To insert a document link highlight your chosen text and press the ‘Insert Document Link’ button to open a pop-up.
    2. Next, scroll through or use the search bar within the ‘Select a document’ pop-up to find your specific document/file and select it.
    3. Insert it using the blue ‘Use document' button towards the bottom left.
    4. To upload a document via this pop-up, click the ‘Upload a file…’ button, then complete the above steps from B to C.

  20. Insert Call-to-Action: Press this button to insert a prepared CTA:
    1. To create a CTA please refer to chapter 3.
    2. To insert a CTA, place your text cursor in the desired module location and press the ‘Insert Call-to-Action’ button to open a pop-up.
    3. Next, scroll through or use the search bar within the ‘Select Call-to-Action’ pop-up to find your specific CTA and select it.
    4. Insert it using the blue ‘Insert CTA’ button towards the bottom left.
    5. To upload an image via this pop-up, click the ‘Create a CTA’ button, then refer to chapter 3.

  21. Insert Read More Separator: Press this button to insert a separator line:
      1. The ‘read more separator line’ is used in blog posts to create your article’s ‘previewed text’, anything above the line is shown in a preview pane, while anything below the line is shown within the main blog page.

    Note, when editing email content the ‘Insert Read More Separator’ icon is replaced with the ‘Insert Personalization Token’ icon (shown below).



  22. Insert Personalization Token: Press this button to insert a token for contact personalization:

    1. To insert a personalization token, place your text cursor in the desired module location and press the ‘Insert Personalization token’ button.

    2. This will now open an ‘Insert Personalization token’ pop-up.
    3. Next click the drop down menu for ‘Category’ and choose the appropriate category.
    4. Then scroll through or use the search bar within the ‘Token’ box to find your contact property and select it.
    5. Insert it using the blue ‘Insert’ button towards the bottom left, or cancel the process by clicking the ‘Cancel’ button next to it.
