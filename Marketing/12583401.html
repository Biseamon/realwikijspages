Creating an A/B test - HubSpot:

 1. Changing a HubSpot 'Website Page' into a 'Landing Page' :

 

  • To perform an A/B Test, your HubSpot page must be a 'Landing Page'.

  • To change a website page into a landing page, find your page in 'Website Pages' > click the dropdown menu for ‘more’ (located towards the far right) > choose ‘Move to landing pages’.

  • A popup will then appear and state a condition of the move, if okay, press the ‘Move to Landing Pages’ button to finish.


  2. Setting up a HubSpot Landing Page A/B Test:

  • Click on your Landing Page (via 'Landing Pages) > click 'Edit' > click 'Settings' > click 'Create and A/B Test' - give your variation a (easy and accurate) name > click 'Okay'
    • This process also works if you've created a new Landing Page.
  • After this change, you'll now see a green/blue 'A/B' icon next to the top-left text for 'Back to landing pages'.
    • Clicking either of these 'A' or 'B' buttons allows you to switch your view between either page.
    • Essentially, treat each page as a seperate entitity, and make your specifc changes. To learn more about creating a landing page, please see the HubSpot Wiki.
    • Note, changes made to one page should not affect the other - unless it's a change to a global template module which your two pages share.
  • To publish a variation, click the blue 'Publish' button on the top-right of each page.
  • To stop or choose a variation winner, click on your Landing Page (via 'Landing Pages) > click 'Variations' > click 'Choose as winner'.

 

 3. Enabling Google Analytics A/B Tracking:

  • To enable Google Analytics tracking, each variation will need a specific piece of code inserted into it's header. 
  • To do this for your 'A' page > open you 'A' page and click 'Settings' > click 'Edit HTML settings' > insert '<meta name="bbmarketing-abtest" content="A">' at the top of the entry box, and before any other code (if present).
  • To do this for your 'B' page > open you 'A' page and click 'Settings' > click 'Edit HTML settings' > insert '<meta name="bbmarketing-abtest" content="B">' at the top of the entry box, and before any other code (if present).
  • To remove this code, simply delete it from the header file.
  • After 1-2 days (after GA has recorded some traffic), these results will show in GA with the end of their URL showing '/?abtest=A' and '/?abtest=A' respectively.
    • Note, I would advise to only have this code implemented across one A/B test at a time. However, if you wish to run additional tests, I would contact Alex Putnam and ask him to adapt the code.
