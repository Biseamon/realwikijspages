Blueberry : Ch. 1.2 - Side Bar Icons

Created by Unknown User (mct), last modified on Jul 07, 2016

At the left-hand side of your page you’ll see 'side bar' icons for the following:

These include (descending in order):

  • All Posts: This is a backlink to the main directory for your content type.
  • Preview
    • Device preview - view the page as your audience via several devices types.
    • Smart preview – choose a contact from the drop-down menu and view your content as if you were them, useful for when utilising smart content.
    • Shareable preview – create a preview link that you can share with others.
  • Optimise: An important section that helps your content rank within SERP's - explained further below.
  • Edit Modules: An alternative way to edit your content rather than clicking within the module itself - explained further below.

Please note when editing email content some additional icons will also appear, but these are explained further within chapter 7 for emails.

Optimise

To help your content rank within search engine results pages (SERP's), HubSpot's ‘Optimise’ feature will analyse your content for effective keyword dispersal.

To use this feature, simply place your text cursor into the entry bar and submit your chosen terms (from targeted keyword research), or you can choose from a list of previously used keywords by searching through the suggestions list. However, please try to avoid repeating keywords (if possible), as this can sometimes cause your own content to compete against each other.

Try to use a minimum of two keywords for your content. Once these have been entered a ‘To-Do’ list of four sections will appear (as bulleted below), and each section will be broken down to display where additional keyword information is needed.

  • Content Body
  • Title
  • Meta
  • Image

Note, these sections may vary in order depending on the completion rate of your content.

By default, incomplete to-do’s will be highlighted in orange to start with, but as your content is progressively entered the to-do’s will change from orange to green. So keep an eye on them while entering your text and amend any issues that crop up.

Edit Modules

The 3D square icon for ‘Edit modules’ provides an alternative way to enter or edit your page's module text (shown below).

By clicking on this icon and choosing the module you want to edit, two tabs will appear called ‘Options’ and ‘Styles’ (shown below). Simply click inside the ‘Options’ tab to enter your text, or use the ‘Styles’  tab to adjust your styling rules.
